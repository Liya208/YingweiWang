**Yingwei Wang**, Associate Professor, from Zhoukou, Henan Province. Recipient of the Hunan Provincial Excellent Youth Fund (2022) and Shenghua Scholar of Central South University (2024).

He received his Ph.D. in Physics from Central South University in 2017. Subsequently, he conducted postdoctoral research at Shenzhen University (in collaboration with Profs. Han Zhang and Guoping Wang), Jinan University (in collaboration with Profs. Xiangping Li and Qiaoliang Bao), and the College of Science, Korea University (in collaboration with Prof. Jong Seung Kim). His research focuses on ultrafast nonlinear optics of low-dimensional materials, and he has made significant progress in the study of ultrafast nonlinear optical effects and their modulation in such materials. He has published 53 SCI papers as first author or corresponding author (including co-first and co-corresponding) in renowned international journals such as *Chemical Society Reviews*, *Nano Letters* (2 papers), *ACS Nano*, *Opto-Electronic Advances* (2 papers), *Advanced Functional Materials*, *Advanced Science*, etc. His work has been cited over 3800 times on Google Scholar, with an h-index of 31. He has been invited to deliver more than 20 plenary or invited talks at international and domestic conferences, including the Asia-Pacific Laser Symposium (ALS), LTO, and CIOP.

He has led six vertical research projects at the national, provincial, and municipal levels, including the NSFC Youth Fund, the Hunan Provincial Excellent Youth Fund, and two General Grants (First-class funding) from the China Postdoctoral Science Foundation. He has also undertaken two horizontal projects for the defense industry. He received the Second Prize of the Hunan Provincial Natural Science Award (3/6), as well as the First Prize (1/5) and Second Prize (2/5) of the Hunan Optical Science and Technology Progress Award. He is a member of several professional societies including the Chinese Optical Society, the Chinese Physical Society, the Hunan Optical Society, and the Hunan Physical Society. He serves as a member of the Committee on Light-Matter Interactions under the Chinese Society for Optical Engineering, a youth committee member of the Hunan Optical Society, and a youth editorial board member for *Nano-Micro Letters* (JCR Q1; IF=36.3), *Journal of Central South University* (JCR Q1; IF=2.39), *Infrared and Laser Engineering*, and *Acta Photonica Sinica*.

He is the Head of the Optics Group of the Hunan Provincial Key Laboratory of Ultra-Microstructure and Ultrafast Processes, and a core member of the Hunan Provincial Key Laboratory of Nanophotonics and Devices. The Principal Investigator of the "Ultrafast Nonlinear Optics" research group at the School of Physics, Central South University, is Professor Jun He, who is also the Vice President of Central South University, a recipient of the NSFC Excellent Young Scientist Fund, and the Director of the Hunan Provincial Key Laboratory of Nanophotonics and Devices. The group focuses on nonlinear optical effects and ultrafast phenomena in novel nanoscale materials, optically induced and controlled spin polarization in semiconductor nanostructures, and low-dimensional semiconductor materials and devices.

**Welcome undergraduate students, master's students, doctoral students, and postdoctoral fellows to join our research group!!!** 

The group fosters a strong scientific research atmosphere: rigorous and focused during work, brainstorming during group meetings, and collaborative problem-solving in the face of challenges. There is no unhealthy internal competition, only mutual support, allowing everyone to delve deeply into research and grow in a relaxed and pleasant environment.
